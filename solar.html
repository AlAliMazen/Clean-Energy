Solar Energy

Unleashing the power of the sun, solar energy illuminates a sustainable tomorrow

How it work?

The process of using solar energy involves collecting solar radiation and turning it into heat or useful electricity. The two primary methods of harnessing solar energy are Photovoltaic (PV) systems for electricity generation and Solar thermal systems for heat production.

Solar System Components

A - Photovoltaic (PV) systems

Following Components are the common parts but not all
Element Description
Solar Panels (Photovoltaic Cells) Individual silicon or other semiconductor-based solar cells make up photovoltaic (PV) modules. By using the photovoltaic effect, these cells turn sunlight into electricity.
Inverter A key part of PV systems is the inverter. It transforms the solar panels' direct current (DC) into alternating current (AC), the common type of electricity utilised in homes and businesses.
Mounting Structure A framework provides support for the solar panels, holding them in place and facilitating their optimal orientation and tilt to maximise sunshine exposure. Mounting structures come in three varieties: fixed, movable, and sun-tracking tracking systems.
Batteries Batteries are frequently a part of off-grid solar systems in order to store extra electricity produced during sunny spells. These batteries hold energy so they can be used at night or on overcast days, when sunshine isn't available.
Monitoring and Control Systems Real-time data on the solar energy system's operation, including energy generation, system efficiency, and possible problems, is provided by monitoring devices. Control systems can automate specific tasks to maximise energy harvesting and system efficiency.

B - Solar thermal systems

Following Components are the common parts but not all
Element Description
Solar Thermal Collectors Solar thermal collectors are used in solar thermal systems to capture sunlight and convert it into heat. These collectors come in various types, including flat-plate collectors, concentrating collectors, and evacuated tube collectors.
Heat Exchanger In solar thermal systems, a heat exchanger transfers the heat collected by the solar thermal collectors to a fluid, typically water or a heat-transfer fluid. This heated fluid is then used for various applications, such as space heating or generating steam for electricity production.
Pump Solar thermal systems may include a pump to circulate the heat-transfer fluid between the collector and the heat exchanger. This ensures a continuous and efficient transfer of heat.
