Hydro Energy

Our transition to a sustainable future is made possible by the grace of hydroelectric ingenuity, which harnesses the force of rushing rivers

How it work?

Hydroelectric power is generated by harnessing the energy from flowing or falling water to produce electricity.

Hydropower Components

Following Components are the common parts but not all
Element Description
Dam Crest The top of the dam, where water is impounded. The crest is designed to withstand the water pressure and other loads.
Spillway (Emergency channel) An engineered structure that allows excess water to flow safely over or around the dam during periods of heavy rainfall or flooding.
Abutments The natural terrain or constructed structures on the sides of the dam that support and anchor it.
Foundation The base on which the dam rests. It must be stable and able to withstand the forces exerted by the dam and the water it holds.
Upstream Face The side of the dam facing the reservoir. It is designed to withstand the water pressure and is often more massive than the downstream face.
Downstream Face The side of the dam facing away from the reservoir. It is typically sloped and may be less massive than the upstream face.
Reservoir The body of water created by the dam, used for various purposes like water supply, recreation, or hydroelectric power generation.
Access Roads and Maintenance For accessing the dam for inspections, maintenance, and emergency response.
