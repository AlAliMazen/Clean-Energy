Wind Energy

Capturing the whispers of the wind, our journey toward a cleaner tomorrow unfolds with the grace of wind energy

How it work?

Wind turbines work by converting the kinetic energy of the wind into mechanical energy, which is then transformed into electrical energy.

Wind Turbine Components

Following Components are the common parts but not all
Element Description
Rotor Blades Long, aerodynamic blades are mounted on the rotor hub. These blades capture the kinetic energy of the wind.
Rotor Hub The rotor hub connects the blades to the main shaft and allows them to rotate. It is positioned at the top of the tower.
Generator The generator converts the mechanical energy from the rotating blades into electrical energy.
Gearbox a gearbox is used to increase the rotational speed of the rotor to the higher speed needed for the generator.
Tower The tower supports the entire structure and elevates the rotor hub to a higher position.
Controller The controller monitors data from sensors and controls the turbine's operations, adjusting the pitch (turn point) of the blades for optimal performance and safety.
Brakes Mechanical or electrical brakes are installed to stop the rotor in case of emergencies or during maintenance
